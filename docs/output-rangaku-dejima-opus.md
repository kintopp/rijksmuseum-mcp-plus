## 2. üë§ KEY FIGURES ‚Äî SIEBOLD AND THE BAUDUIN BROTHERS

### Philipp Franz von Siebold on Deshima (`NG-1978-111`)
- **Signed by Obunsai**, 1823‚Äì1830 | *Kakemono* (hanging scroll), brush on silk
- Depicts **Von Siebold** ‚Äî the German physician in VOC service and pivotal figure in Rangaku ‚Äî **dictating a letter** to a scribe on Deshima, with a Javanese servant, a boy, and what may be his medical assistant.
- Siebold was *oppermeester* (chief physician) on Deshima from 1823‚Äì1830, and his scientific exchanges with Japanese scholars were central to the flow of Western knowledge into Japan.

### Bauduin Brothers on Dejima (`RP-F-2016-76-34`)
- **Albumen print**, 1862‚Äì1866, attributed to Antoon Bauduin
- Group portrait of seven men in a **half-open photographic studio in a garden on Dejima** ‚Äî among the earliest photographs taken there.
- Curatorial note: *"Letters Albert sent home from Japan are our main source of information about the brothers' lives in Japan."*

### Photograph: View of Dejima and Nagasaki (`RP-F-2016-76-37`)
- **Albumen print**, c. 1865‚Äì1870 | From the Bauduin collection
- Bird's-eye view showing Dejima in the centre of Nagasaki ‚Äî part of the emerging market for **photographic souvenirs** of Japan.

## 7. üîç RESEARCH RECOMMENDATIONS FOR THE RANGAKU SCHOLAR

1. **Start with the physical model** (`NG-721`) and **Kawahara Keiga paintings** (`NG-1190`, `NG-1191`) ‚Äî these provide the most detailed visual documentation of Deshima's layout and daily life during the peak Rangaku period (1820s‚Äì1840s).

2. **The lacquer panel of chief factors** (`BK-16465`) is an invaluable prosopographical source ‚Äî a complete list of Dutch directors 1610‚Äì1850 with ship counts.

3. **The Siebold kakemono** (`NG-1978-111`) is directly relevant to Rangaku: it shows the very practice of knowledge exchange ‚Äî a Dutch physician dictating, with Japanese and Javanese intermediaries present.

4. **The Nagasaki-e print** (`RP-P-1956-461`) and **Sadahide woodcut** (`NG-663-3`) show how Japanese artists visualized the Dutch ‚Äî essential for understanding the Japanese reception side of Rangaku.

5. **The anti-foreign porcelain dish** (`NG-1979-19-1`) with its Tenp≈ç-era inscription provides a counter-narrative: evidence of the xenophobic sentiment that existed *alongside* Rangaku curiosity.

6. **The Japan (collectie) curated set** (set `26134`) contains **7,735 objects** and is worth systematic browsing for additional connections.

7. Note that three key Deshima objects are **currently on display** in the "Netherlands Overseas" gallery (HG-1.17): the model, and both lacquer panels. All images are available at high resolution under a CC0 public domain license, making them freely usable for scholarly publication.
